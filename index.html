Best Practices Team

Daniel Siepmann, Oliver Klee, Łukasz Uznański

Best Practices Team

Our purpose ("Why?")

We help TYPO3 developers build high-quality extensions.

Best Practices Team

Our mission ("What?")

  • We keep evaluating and adding new helpful tools and examples for best practices to the Tea example extension.
  • Also, we keep current with new versions of PHP, TYPO3, Symfony etc.
  • We also build and maintain a project builder template for TYPO3 extensions.

Best Practices Team

What's in it for you?

  • You will learn how to ensure high quality for extensions
  • Help others to build high quality extensions
  • Support docs.typo3.org with tested code examples

Best Practices Team

Requirements for joining the team

  • You have reviewed 2 PRs by the current team members.
  • You have got 2 of your PRs in our projects merged.
  • You have been present in 3 consecutive team calls.
  • You express your wish to join our team, to further our mission, accept the team rules, and are willing to invest some hours per month for this team.

Data Protection Team

Our purpose ("Why?")

As the data protection officer, I keep an eye on the processes in the TYPO3 Association and hold training sessions for the team members.

Data Protection Team

Our mission ("What?")

I keep the data protection documents up to date, train the team members and the TYPO3 Board in data protection, GDPR and the Swiss data protection and have an open ear for process assessments.

Data Protection Team

Looking for multipliers and contacts from other teams

In order to always keep an eye on the processes and programs used in the other teams in terms of data protection, contact persons and a transparent exchange are important.

Data Protection Team

Data Protection Team

Documentation Team

Our purpose ("Why?")

Up-to-date documentation is important for a product like TYPO3.

Documentation Team

Our mission ("What?")

  • Comprehensive, relevant, up-to-date and easy to find documentation about TYPO3 products for all users.
  • Provide the TYPO3 community with the tools to improve documentation.
  • Provide information about how to write documentation.
  • Provide infrastructure and support to help the TYPO3 community write great documentation.

Documentation Team

What's in it for you?

  • Writing documentation and reviewing pull requests is a way to learn more about the different parts of TYPO3.
  • Improve your English, if you are not a native speaker.

Documentation Team

Requirements for joining the team

  • You should be able to dedicate time to working on the docs regularly and take responsibility for providing effective documentation for the community.
  • Optional: You are interested in the tools and want to improve them with PHP or frontend/JavaScript development.

Localization Team

Peter Kraume, Abdulhamid Kwieder, Preben Rather Sørensen, Georg Ringer, Xavier Perseguers, Josef Glatz

Localization Team

Our purpose ("Why?")

We want to help users of TYPO3 to translate the TYPO3 backend and TYPO3 extensions into their language.

Localization Team

Our mission ("What?")

  • We help to make the localization process as smooth as possible.
  • We take part in helping with errors and bugs in Crowdin or TYPO3 core related to localization.
  • We help developers create projects on the Crowdin server.
  • We maintain the localization ecosystem.

Localization Team

What's in it for you?

  • Learn about the challenges of translation and localization.
  • Another way to contribute to TYPO3.
  • You help non-English speaking users to use TYPO3 in their own language.

Localization Team

Requirements for joining the team

  • You don't necessarily need to have any coding skills to be able to participate.
  • You have attended at least two team meetings.
  • You want to give your time on a regular basis.

Motivation Research Team

Oliver Klee, Sabine Schmidt, Yasmin Haustein,
Marcel Ackermann, Davyd Rabinovych

Motivation Research Team

Our purpose ("Why?")

We want to make work in volunteer communities (like the TYPO3 community) more engaging, motivating and fun. This helps those communities grow and thrive.

It also contributes to building trust and a feeling of connection between the members.

Motivation Research Team

Our mission ("What?")

  • becoming experts on motivation psychology
    and gamification
  • providing advice to other teams (if requested)
  • building ready-to-use tools to enhance motivation

Motivation Research Team

What's in it for you?

  • You learn a lot about motivation psychology and gamification, which you can use in your job.
  • You get to nerd out on game design and psychology together with like-minded people.
  • You will have a good time in a very friendly,
    high-performance team with a high degree of psychological safety.

Motivation Research Team

Requirements for joining the team

  • You want to become an expert on motivation psychology and gamification.
  • You like playing computer games or board games.
  • You are willing to invest at least 4 hours per month into the team.
  • You are fine with our team culture and team rules.

Server Team

Andri Steiner, Andreas Beutel, Bastian Bringenberg, Michael Stucki, Stephan Großberndt

Server Team

Our purpose ("Why?")

Coordinate and run the applications in use within the TYPO3 community.

Server Team

Our mission ("What?")

  • We manage the infrastructure required (for example: servers, domains)
  • We run about a dozen core applications (for example: user directory, bug tracker, ticket system)
  • We provide our services to other teams for their own use (for example: typo3.org)
  • We serve as central contact point in case of any questions related to typo3.org servers and websites (for example: login problems)

Server Team

What's in it for you?

  • Run (open source) software on a large scale
  • Share knowledge and best practices with industry experts

Server Team

Requirements for joining the team

  • You don't need to have any DevOps skills to be able to participate
  • However, you shouldn't be afraid of DevOps basics (mostly Git & containers nowadays)
  • You want to give your time regularly (monthly coordination meetings)

Translation Handling Initiative

Eric Harrer, Sven Wappler, Jo Hasenau,
Astrid Haubold, André Buchmann

Translation Handling Initiative

Our purpose ("Why?")

We are a strategic core development initiative with the aim of simplifying, improving and professionalizing the translation process in TYPO3.

Translation Handling Initiative

Our mission ("What?")

  • Support the core development team
  • Fixes and improvements to the core
  • Identifying community needs
  • Translation process documentation
  • Strategy development/implementation

Translation Handling Initiative

What's in it for you?

  • Deepening your understanding of translation handling processes in TYPO3
  • Develop strategies for medium- and long-term changes to the core
  • Play your part in sustainable core improvements
  • Exchange with core development team members

Translation Handling Initiative

Requirements for joining the team

  • You have experience with multilingual TYPO3 projects as editor, integrator, developer, translator or other person concerned
  • You are interested in TYPO3's internal translation handling processes
  • You want to give your time regularly (weekly meetings)

Translation Handling Initiative
